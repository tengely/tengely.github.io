ExerciseÂ 3.3.3 An integer solution to the equation \(4x_1+5x_2=1\) is

\begin{equation*} (x_1,x_2)=(-1,1)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Hence we have a particular solution

\begin{equation*} (x_1,x_2)=(-n,n) \end{equation*}

in case of the equation \(4x_1+5x_2=n\text{.}\) It is now clear that all solutions can be obtained from the parametrization

\begin{equation*} (-n-5t,n+4t) t\in\mathbb{Z}\text{.} \end{equation*}

To have non-negative solutions one needs

\begin{align*} -n-5t\amp \geq 0\Rightarrow t\leq \frac{-n}{5}\\ n+4t\amp \geq 0\Rightarrow t\geq \frac{-n}{4}\text{.} \end{align*}

Thus we have

\begin{equation*} -\frac{n}{4}\leq t\leq -\frac{n}{5}\text{.} \end{equation*}
