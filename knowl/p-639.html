It is quite clear that by increasing \(m\text{,}\) we would have harder and harder time to calculate the obtained sums. Nevertheless, only by computing the first couple sums we can make a guess at the general answer:

\begin{align*} \amp \text{ for \(m=0\) } \amp \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{k}{0} \amp = n+1,\\ \amp \text{ for \(m=1\) } \amp \sum_{k=1}^n \binom{k}{1} \amp = \frac{(n+1) \cdot n}{2},\\ \amp \text{ for \(m=2\) } \amp \sum_{k=2}^n \binom{k}{2} \amp = \frac{(n+1) \cdot n \cdot (n-1)}{3 \cdot 2}\text{.} \end{align*} in-context
