ExerciseÂ 4.2.1 If \(n\) is odd, then every binomial coefficient occurs twice in the sum: once with positive sign, and once with negative sign. Indeed, the sign of \(\binom{n}{k}\) is \((-1)^k\) and the sign of \(\binom{n}{n-k}\) is \((-1)^{n-k}\text{.}\) They cannot have the same sign, because their product is \((-1)^k \cdot (-1)^{n-k} = (-1)^n = -1\text{,}\) as \(n\) is odd. Thus every binomial coefficient occurs twice with different signs, their sum is 0, and the whole sum is 0, as well.
