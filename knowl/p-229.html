Note, that permutations may arise in many situation. Recall that at the beginning of ChapterÂ 2, Alice, Beth, Claire, Diane, Ed, Frank, George and Hugo wanted to form four dancing couples. Then Alice chose a partner first, then Beth, then Claire, and finally Diane. That is, their choosing put an order on the four boys, and therefore determined a permutation of them. And indeed, there are \(4!=24\) permutations of the four boys, and they can form four dancing couples in \(4!=24\)-many ways, as well.
