The following calculation shows that the two sides are equal:

\begin{align*} \binom{n}{k} \amp = \frac{n!}{k! \cdot (n-k)!} = \frac{n \cdot (n-1) \cdot \dots \cdot (n-k+1) \cdot (n-k)!}{k! \cdot (n-k)!}\\ \amp = \frac{n \cdot (n-1) \cdot \dots \cdot (n-k+1)}{k!}\text{.} \end{align*} in-context
