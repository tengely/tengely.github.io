Exercise 2.1.8 We apply Theorem 2.1.4. Now, we allow spaces, thus the alphabet contains 27 letters. There are \(27^{20}\) possibilities for a 20 letter long string (name), 2 possibilities for the gender, \(27^{10}\) possibilities for a 10 letter long string (job title), and \(10^8\) possibilities for an at most 8 digit long base 10 number (payment). Thus, the number of possibilities is

\begin{equation*} 27^{20} \cdot 2 \cdot 27^{10} \cdot 10^{8}\text{.} \end{equation*}
