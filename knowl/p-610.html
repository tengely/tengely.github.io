Let us use a similar reasoning to calculate the sum of the numbers in a row, with alternating signs. That is, compute the sum

\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=0}^n \left( -1 \right)^k \cdot \binom{n}{k} = \binom{n}{0} - \binom{n}{1} + \binom{n}{2} - \dots + \left( -1 \right)^{n-1} \cdot \binom{n}{n-1} + \left( -1 \right)^{n} \cdot \binom{n}{n}\text{.} \end{equation*} in-context
