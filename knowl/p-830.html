Exercise 2.7.6 Let every pirate take one gold piece right at the very beginning. Then there remains \(k-n\) gold pieces to further distribute. Moreover, now every pirate needs one more gold piece. Thus we reduced the problem to the one solved in Theorem 2.7.2: \(n\) pirates want to distribute \(k-n\) gold pieces such that everyone gets at least one gold piece. By Theorem 2.7.2 this can be done in \(\binom{k-n-1}{n-1}\)-many ways.
