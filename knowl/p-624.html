Finally, let us rewrite the left hand side by using the symmetry of Pascal's triangle, that is, \(\binom{n}{n-k} = \binom{n}{k}\) to obtain the left hand side of (4.2.2):

\begin{align*} \amp \binom{n}{0} \cdot \binom{n}{n} + \binom{n}{1} \cdot \binom{n}{n-1} + \dots + \binom{n}{n}\cdot \binom{n}{0} = \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} \cdot \binom{n}{n-k}\\ \amp = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k}^2 = \binom{n}{0}^2 + \binom{n}{1}^2 + \dots + \binom{n}{n-1}^2 + \binom{n}{n}^2\text{.} \end{align*} in-context
