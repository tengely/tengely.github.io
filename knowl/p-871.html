We checked that \(a_n=\frac{3}{2}\cdot 2^n+2\cdot (-1)^n\) for \(n\in\halmaz{1,2,3,4,5}\text{.}\) Assume that the statement is true for \(S(k-1)\) and \(S(k)\) for some \(2\leq k\in\mathbb{N}\text{,}\) that is,

\begin{align*} a_{k-1}\amp =\frac{3}{2}\cdot 2^{k-1}+2\cdot (-1)^{k-1},\\ a_k\amp =\frac{3}{2}\cdot 2^k+2\cdot (-1)^k\text{.} \end{align*}

The statement for \(k+1\) is that \(a_{k+1}=\frac{3}{2}\cdot 2^{k+1}+2\cdot (-1)^{k+1}\text{.}\) By definition we have that

\begin{equation*} a_{k+1}=a_{k}+2a_{k-1}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Hence by the induction hypotheses we obtain

\begin{equation*} a_{k+1}=\frac{3}{2}\cdot 2^k+2\cdot (-1)^k+2\left(\frac{3}{2}\cdot 2^{k-1}+2\cdot (-1)^{k-1}\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}

A direct computation yields that

\begin{equation*} a_{k+1}=\frac{3}{2}\cdot 2^{k+1}+2\cdot (-1)^{k+1}\text{,} \end{equation*}

which is the statement we wanted to prove. We showed that the given formula is correct.
