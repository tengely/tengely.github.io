Now, consider \((x+y)^3\text{.}\) This is the threefold product of \((x+y)\) with itself, that is, \((x+y)^3 = (x+y)\cdot (x+y) \cdot (x+y)\text{.}\) Note, that this is the same as the product \((x+y)^2 \cdot (x+y)\text{,}\) for which the first factor we have already computed in (4.1.1).

\begin{align*} (x+y)^3 \amp = (x+y)^2 \cdot (x+y) = (x^2 + 2xy + y^2)\cdot (x+y)\\ \amp = x^3 + x^2y + 2x^2y + 2xy^2 + xy^2 + y^3\\ \amp = x^3 + 3x^2y + 3 xy^2 + y^3\text{.} \end{align*} in-context
