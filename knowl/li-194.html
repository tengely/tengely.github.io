Let \(S(n)\) be the statement that \(a_n\leq 2\text{.}\) Our computations show that \(S(1)\) is true. Assume that \(S(k)\) is true for some \(k\geq 1\text{,}\) that is, \(a_k\leq 2\text{.}\) We consider the statement \(S(k+1)\text{.}\) By definition of the sequence we have

\begin{equation*} a_{k+1}=\sqrt{2+a_k}\text{.} \end{equation*}

By the induction hypothesis \(a_k\leq 2\text{,}\) hence

\begin{equation*} a_{k+1}\leq \sqrt{2+2}=2\text{.} \end{equation*}

The statement \(S(k+1)\) has been proved and thus we have that \(a_n\leq 2\) for all \(n\in\mathbb{N}\text{.}\)
